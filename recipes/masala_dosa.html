Masala Dosa

Description :

Masala dosa are crispy, soft, savory and healthy crepes made with a rice and lentil batter. The dosa are gluten-free and dairy-free, with a sturdy yet soft texture that's great for holding a hearty homemade potato filling. To make the batter, lentils and rice are soaked for several hours and then blended together.

Dosa is high in carbohydrates and contains no added sugars or saturated fats. As its key ingredients are rice and black gram, it is a good source of protein. A typical homemade plain dosa without oil contains about 112 calories, of which 84% is carbohydrate and 16% protein.

Another tale narrates that it was one of the kings of Mysore, Someshvara III, who was the inventor of the masala dosa. The king had hosted an extravagant party that served a lot of food to his guests. However, once the party had come to an end, the king noticed that there were a lot of food left behind. Since he didn't want the food to go to waste, he called in his team of cooks to come up with something creative. The cooks set out to work by combining leftover potatoes with spices and then used as a filling for the plain dosa. However, the cooks would add a layer of spicy red chutney beneath the spiced potatoes and then fold the crepe and serve it with a side of plain coconut chutney.

Ingredients for pure vegetarian tasty dish :)

  • Rice
  • Lentils
  • Urad dal (black gram beans)
  • Fenugreek
  • Puffed rice
  • Potato curry
  • Chutneys
  • Sambar

Steps :

  1. Soak 1 cup of rice and 1/2 cup of urad dal (split black lentils) separately in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the water from both the rice and dal. Grind them separately to a smooth paste using a blender or wet grinder.
  3. Mix the rice and dal batter together in a large bowl. Add salt to taste and mix well. Let the batter ferment for 8-10 hours or overnight, allowing it to rise and develop a tangy flavor.
  4. For the potato masala filling, heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then, add 1/2 teaspoon of cumin seeds, a pinch of asafoetida (hing), and a few curry leaves.
  5. Add 2 finely chopped green chilies and 1 finely chopped onion. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown.
  6. Add 2 boiled and mashed potatoes to the pan. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  7. Sprinkle 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste. Mix all the ingredients and cook for another 2 minutes. Turn off the heat and set the potato masala aside.
  8. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet on medium heat. Grease it lightly with oil.
  9. Take a ladleful of the dosa batter and pour it onto the center of the skillet. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin, round dosa.
  10. Drizzle a teaspoon of oil around the edges of the dosa and a little over the top. Cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy.
  11. Spoon a generous amount of the potato masala filling onto one side of the dosa. Fold the other side over the filling to form a semi-circle or roll it up into a cylinder
  12. Remove the masala dosa from the skillet and serve it hot with sambar (right most in the picture) and coconut chutney (middle bowl in the picture).
Finally the hot and tasty dish is ready to be served to elevate the soul and fill the soul with joy !!
