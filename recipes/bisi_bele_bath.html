Bisi Bele Bath

Description :

Another lesser known, highly underrated and nutritious-densely packed tasty food :)

  • It is very convenient to eat.
  • It fills you up.
  • It is rich in proteins, which means it helps build muscle.
  • The chili in it speeds up your metabolism and helps you lose weight.
  • The spices used for cooking Bisi bele baths are good for your digestive system and boost immunity.
  • Bisi bele bath is a perfect food for the winter months because it balances stomach heat.
  • Bisi bele bath can get rid of coughs and colds.
  • Bisi bele bath is a delicious dish that can make you feel better in the winter season.

Now the ingredients :

  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1 finely chopped onion
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons Bisi Bele Bath powder
  • Salt to taste

    • For the tempering :

    • 1 tablespoon ghee
    • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
    • 1 dry red chili
    • A few curry leaves
    • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
    • Serve with: Raita

Finally the steps :

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil in a pressure cooker or large pot.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then, add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, a pinch of asafoetida (hing), and a few curry leaves.
  3. Add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until it turns translucent.
  4. Add 1 cup of mixed vegetables like carrots, peas, beans, and bell peppers. Sauté for a few minutes.
  5. Rinse 1 cup of rice and 1/2 cup of toor dal (split pigeon peas) and add them to the pot.
  6. Add 3 cups of water, 2 tablespoons of Bisi Bele Bath powder (readily available spice mix), salt to taste, and mix well.
  7. Close the pressure cooker lid or cover the pot and cook for 3-4 whistles in the pressure cooker or until the rice and dal are cooked and tender.
  8. Once the pressure is released, open the lid and give it a good stir. The consistency should be slightly thick and porridge-like.
  9. For the tempering, heat 1 tablespoon of ghee in a small pan. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then, add 1 dry red chili, a few curry leaves, and a pinch of asafoetida (hing).
  10. Pour the tempering over the Bisi Bele Bath and mix well.
  11. Let the flavors meld for a few minutes before serving.
  12. Serve the hot and flavorful Bisi Bele Bath with a side of raita.

Enjoy your delicious Bisi Bele Bath, a comforting and wholesome one-pot meal!
